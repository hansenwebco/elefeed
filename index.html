Tidy Feeds

Feeds your way

Switch effortlessly between posts from people you follow or curated feeds of your favorite hashtags. Elefeed keeps your timeline organized and focused on what matters most to you.

Every post in your feed is color-coded and clearly notated. Whether it's a hashtag you follow or a profile recommendation, you'll never wonder why a post appeared in your timeline.

Always with you

Mobile-First Experience

Elefeed is built for the modern web. Install it as a Progressive Web App (PWA) to get a native-app feel on your iPhone or Android. Fast, responsive, and ready for your home screen.

Privacy First

Private, local, and fast

Elefeed is a lightweight client that runs entirely in your browser. No databases, no tracking, and no third-party servers. All your data stays in your browser's local storage.

Custom Experience

Tailored to You

Customize every aspect of your experience. Control media previews, filter by language, manage instant notifications, and switch between light and dark modes. Elefeed is built to adapt perfectly to your flow.

Elefeed is open source. Contribute or view the code on GitHub.

elefeed Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy outlines the data processing practices of Elefeed ("the Application").

1. Information Collection and Processing

The Application does not collect, process, transmit, or retain any personally identifiable information, usage metrics, diagnostic data, or activity logs on external network infrastructure or proprietary servers.

2. Third-Party Analytics and Tracking

The Application strictly prohibits the use of third-party analytics services, external tracking mechanisms, embedded scripts, or tracking cookies. Continuous or aggregated user behavior monitoring is not performed.

3. Local Data Storage and Authentication

All operational data required for the Application's functionality, including but not limited to authentication credentials (such as OAuth access tokens) and user-defined application preferences, are maintained exclusively within the Local Storage API of the user's web browser environment. Such data is utilized solely for the purpose of authenticating and securely interacting directly with the user's designated Mastodon instance. No application data leaves the client device to interact with servers affiliated with the Application developers.
